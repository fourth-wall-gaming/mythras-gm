# Art-Style Card: <Style Name>

Used by NOVELIZATION.md step 8 to pin the look of a manuscript's
illustrations. Copy this file to `<your-style>.md`, fill in every section, keep
it under ~40 lines. Be concrete: medium, palette, and line rules an image model
can obey, not vibes. This card becomes the preamble in front of every per-scene
image prompt, so the whole novel reads as one illustrated set.

## Medium
<Painting? Ink engraving? Woodcut? Photographic? Name the physical technique and
the era it evokes.>

## Palette and value
<Colour or monochrome? Key value relationships, contrast, where the darks and
lights sit.>

## Line and texture
<Linework, hatching, grain, edge quality, level of detail and where it
concentrates.>

## Composition
<Aspect, framing, single subject vs. tableau, depth, negative space, how the eye
is led.>

## Subject canon
<The world's recurring visual facts the model must get right: anatomy,
architecture, dress, light sources. PLAYER-VISIBLE only -- never GM secrets.>

## Mood
<The emotional register the image should carry.>

## Never do
<Hard prohibitions -- the fastest way to break the look (e.g. colour, lettering
in the image, modern objects, anachronism).>
