## Step 1: Character Concept
Form a broad idea of the character you want to play (e.g. hardened fighter, cunning rogue). This guides later choices such as Race and Class. Need not be elaborate.

## Step 2: Characteristics
Generate the seven Characteristics (STR, CON, SIZ, DEX, INT, POW, CHA) using one of four methods: Dice Roll, Dice Roll Assign, Dice Roll High, or Points Build. See the Racial Characteristics Table for dice ranges and racial averages.

## Step 3: Attributes
Calculate all derived attributes from the Characteristics:
- Action Points (starts at 2; increases with Rank)
- Damage Modifier (STR+SIZ table)
- Experience Modifier (CHA table)
- Healing Rate (CON table)
- Initiative (average of DEX and INT)
- Luck Points (POW table; +1 for humans; +1 per new Rank)
- Magic Points (equal to POW)
- Movement Rate (racial default -- not from Characteristics)
- Hit Points per Location (CON+SIZ table; +1 per location at Ranks 2 and 4)

## Step 4: Skills
Record the Base Level of all Standard Skills (derived from Characteristic sums or multiples). These will be modified later by culture, class, and bonus points. Note the +40% static bonus applied to Customs and Native Tongue.

## Step 5: Race/Culture
Choose a Race (Human, Dwarf, Elf, Gnome, Half-Elf, Half-Orc, or Halfling). Humans must also choose a Culture (Barbarian, Civilized, or Nomadic). Apply racial/cultural Standard and Professional Skill bonuses (Quick Pick or Point Buy: 100 points, 5-15 per skill). Record Alignment (one Ethical + one Moral Code), two or three Passions, and any required Oaths.

## Step 6: Class
Choose a Class (Cleric, Fighter, Magic-User, or Rogue). Apply Class Standard and Professional Skill bonuses (Quick Pick or Point Buy: 100 points, 0-15 per skill, max 3 Professional Skills). Note Class Abilities and Rank requirements (Rank 1 requires any five Class skills at 40%+).

## Step 7: Bonus Skill Points
Distribute a free pool of Skill Points (default 100 for Rank 1, limit +10 per skill) to round out the character. Optionally choose one final new Professional Skill (hobby). May add a new weapon type to Combat Skill. See the Experience Table for higher-Rank point pools and per-skill limits.

## Step 8: Final Steps
- Determine Alignment and Passions from Race/Culture and Class.
- Note starting Abilities from Race and Class.
- Choose one Rank 1 Class Ability (at no EXP Roll cost).
- Copy down starting equipment from the Class write-up.
- Name the character and establish connections with other characters.
