**Passions** represent any deeply held commitment -- a person, organization, ideal, or object. Described by a verb: Desire, Despise, Destroy, Espouse, Fear, Flee, Forswear, Hate, Love, Loyalty to, Protect, Repudiate, Respect, Seek, Subvert, Torment, or Uphold.

**Rating:** 1-100 (or higher). Improved the same way as skills, or modified by GM based on events.

**Starting Passions:**
- Beginning characters may take up to three Passions.
- Each Passion is based on two Characteristics (see Passion Table for base values).
- Apply bonuses in order of importance: +40 to the first, +30 to the second, +20 to the third.
- Example: Distrust (Rogues); Loyal to Queen; Love Family; Defeat Chaos.

**Acquiring new Passions during play:**
- New Passions can arise at any point during a session when circumstances warrant.
- Passions gained mid-session (e.g., 'Hate X' from a dramatic betrayal, or 'Loyalty to X' from taking an Oath) cost NO Experience Rolls -- established immediately at Base Value.
- Passions established during improvement cost 1 Experience Roll to gain at Base Value.

**Class importance:** For some Classes, a Passion must be maintained at a minimum level to retain Class-specific Abilities. Losing a Passion can result in loss of the Class itself.
