**Using Alignment and Passions** -- five ways they influence play:

1. **Augment a skill:** Adds 20% of the Alignment/Passion value to a skill roll when the augmentation is thematically and dramatically relevant.

2. **Drive choices (Standard roll):** Roll against the Alignment or Passion to determine how strongly the character feels. Success = character acts as the Alignment/Passion dictates. Failure = character may act freely.

3. **Oppose another Alignment or Passion (Opposed Roll):** When two Alignments or Passions conflict -- including within the same character (e.g., Love vs. Oath/Loyalty) -- make an Opposed Roll. The more successful result determines the character's action.

4. **Measure depth of commitment:** Higher value = deeper commitment. Compare ratings between characters with similar Alignments/Passions to determine who is more committed.

5. **Resist psychological manipulation or magical domination:** When forced to act contrary to an Alignment or Passion, the character may substitute the Alignment/Passion value for Willpower in the Opposed Roll.
