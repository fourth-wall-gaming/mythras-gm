Spells that require touching an opponent do **not** require an additional Unarmed skill roll -- physical contact is part of the spell's somatic component and is covered by the casting roll.

**Detailed touch-spell procedure:**
- Typically cast and held until the caster can make a successful Unarmed roll to touch an opponent.
- However, if the caster is within reach of a target on the **same Turn** casting finishes, they may touch the target without a separate Unarmed roll (the hit is folded into the casting roll).
- The opponent may **Parry or Evade** normally.
- If contact is made, the target still makes an Opposed Roll to resist as specified in the spell (success by the victim negates adverse effects).
- A Touch spell needs only to contact the target or its worn accoutrements to take effect.
- The caster may always be the beneficiary of their own Touch spell if desired.
