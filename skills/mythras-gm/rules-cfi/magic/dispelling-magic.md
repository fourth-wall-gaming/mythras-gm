Magic is dispelled using **Magnitude** (= caster's skill / 10 at Maximum). When Dispel Magic is cast against an ongoing effect or magic item, there is **no resistance roll** -- instead:

- If the **Intensity of Dispel Magic exceeds the Magnitude** of the target magic, it is dispelled.
- Magic items and Permanent spells are only **temporarily** dispelled.

**Opposed (same or contrary) spells:** Some spells can counter other spells directly (noted in their write-ups). When used this way, each 1 Intensity counts as **3 Intensity** for countering purposes.
- Example: Darkness of Magnitude 6 requires Dispel Magic at Intensity 7, OR Light at only Intensity 3.

**In-play note:** The Magnitude of an existing spell is usually **unknown** -- dispelling is generally trial and error.
