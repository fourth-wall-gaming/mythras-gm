**Intensity** and **Magnitude** measure the potency of a spell:

- **Intensity:** The level at which the spell is cast. Ranges from **1** to the **Maximum Intensity**. Governs the spell's effect (area, damage, duration, etc.) and MP cost. The caster may choose any Intensity between 1 and Maximum.
- **Magnitude:** Always equal to the **Maximum Intensity** (i.e., the caster's Arcane Casting or Divine Channeling skill divided by 10). Governs how difficult the spell is to **dispel or resist**. Magnitude is fixed regardless of chosen Intensity.

**Key rule:** Difficulty Grades applied to the casting roll do NOT alter Maximum Intensity or Magnitude. A caster with 60% skill always has Maximum Intensity 6 and Magnitude 6, even if the roll is Hard or Herculean.

**Example:** With Arcane Casting 56%, Rengarth has Maximum Intensity 6 and Magnitude 6. He may cast Fireball at Intensity 3 (spending fewer MP), but the spell still has Magnitude 6 for resistance and dispel purposes.
