**Neither illusions nor phantasms can break physical laws:**

- An illusory horse cannot be physically mounted (no real weight-bearing).
- An illusory bridge will not let a party cross a real chasm.
- Victims under the spell's influence perceive events as normal (they see themselves riding the horse, crossing the bridge) -- until reality reasserts itself (e.g., impacting the chasm floor).

**Combat and damage (illusions while concentrated upon):**
- Illusory creatures may attack and cause harm that is "real" for wound levels while the illusion persists.
- Damage vanishes as soon as the illusion is revealed.
- The caster determines all hit/damage/Special Effect results; these must remain realistic to avoid easy Disbelief.
- Example: Illusory kobolds that never miss or always sidestep every blow invite Disbelief.

**Believability:** The simpler and more familiar the illusion, the more believable. Something only known from a painting or description will appear less realistic than something the caster personally knows.

**Golems and programmed undead** (skeletons, zombies): Completely immune to both illusions and phantasms -- they lack cognitive reasoning.
