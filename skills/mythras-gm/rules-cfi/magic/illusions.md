An **illusion** is a manifestation created within a defined area. Once cast, it is visible to everyone who enters that area for as long as it remains.

- Can manipulate light, sound, shadow, color, scent. Higher-Rank forms may create quasi-real matter drawn from other planes (real to believers).
- Cannot make something look like nothing (e.g., cannot create true invisibility -- Invisibility is specialized Illusion magic). Can camouflage with fog, alter appearances, etc.
- The Area of Effect defines where the manifestation fits; the illusion itself may be visible from beyond that area (a tower illusion can be seen from a mile away).

**Dispelling by touch:** An illusion is automatically dispelled when physically touched (bare hand, weapon, hurled object) -- unless the caster concentrates and maintains it by spending **1 MP**. Maintaining allows the illusionist to have it react appropriately (e.g., an illusory creature reacting to a hit).

**Disbelief:** Subject to Disbelief (Opposed Willpower vs. original casting roll). Success = viewer sees through it; failure = no effect, no further attempt unless the illusion does something newly suspicious (GM's discretion). Only sapient creatures (INT Characteristic) may attempt to Disbelieve.

**Damage from illusions:** While concentrated upon, illusions may attack and cause illusory damage (only in the minds of believers). Damage is treated as real for wound levels, but vanishes once the illusion is revealed. Caster determines hit, damage, and Special Effects results -- must remain realistic.

**"Killed" by illusion:** Victim collapses. GM makes a secret Willpower roll:
- Fumble: Dies from system shock.
- Failure: Awakens unharmed after 1d6 minutes.
- Success: Awakens unharmed after 1d6 Rounds.
- Critical: Resists losing consciousness; may immediately attempt to Disbelieve.

**Dispel Magic** dispels the manifestation if it overcomes Magnitude. Against a larger illusion, Dispel Magic dispels a portion equal to its own area.
