**To attempt to Disbelieve** an illusion or phantasm, the viewer must:
1. **State their intent** (player declares Disbelief for illusions; GM secretly rolls for phantasms upon exposure).
2. Succeed at an **Opposed Roll: Willpower vs. Arcane Casting or Divine Channeling** of the original spellcaster.

**Results:**
- **Success:** The viewer sees the illusion or phantasm for what it is.
- **Failure:** No effect; no further attempts unless the manifestation does something that newly causes suspicion (GM's discretion, case-by-case).

**Modifiers:**
- Phantasms are typically **2 Difficulty Grades harder** to Disbelieve (rooted in the psyche).
- The more unbelievable the illusion, the easier to Disbelieve; realistic illusions are harder.

**Eligibility:** Only **sapient creatures** (those with the INT Characteristic) may attempt to Disbelieve. Constructs such as golems and programmed undead (skeletons, zombies) are immune to illusions and phantasms entirely.

**Group dynamics:** When some viewers see through the illusion and others do not, each group acts according to their own beliefs. Those who know it is an illusion may attempt to convince others, but those still affected will be convinced the illusion is real and that the others are the ones being manipulated.

Illusions and phantasms may also be dispelled with Dispel Magic (overcoming Magnitude).
