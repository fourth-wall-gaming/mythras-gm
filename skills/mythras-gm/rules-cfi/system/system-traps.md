**Construction:** Small traps (bear traps, trapped locks) require Mechanisms. Large-scale traps (spiked pits, rolling boulders) require Engineering.

**Rough cost guidelines:** Alarms: creator's skill in copper. Ensnaring: silver. Death-traps: gold.

**Trap Traits:**

- **Purpose:** Alarm (signal trigger), Ensnaring (capture), Maiming (critically injure), Death (kill).
- **Trigger:** How the trap is set off.
- **Difficulty:** Equal to the maker's Mechanisms or Engineering skill. Used in Opposed Rolls to spot, evade, or disarm.
- **Resistance:** Brawn, Evade, or a shield-incorporating Combat skill (rolled when trap triggers).
- **Effect:** What happens on a failed resistance. Damage from mechanical death-traps is limited by the maker's skill (see Death-Trap Damage Limits). Exceptions: poison damage and pit trap damage follow their own rules (poison type or fall distance).

**Death-Trap Damage Limits:**

| Maker's Skill | Damage | Size/Force |
|:-------------:|:------:|:----------:|
| 1-10% | 1d2 | Small |
| 11-20% | 1d4 | Small |
| 21-30% | 1d6 | Medium |
| 31-40% | 1d8 | Medium |
| 41-50% | 1d10 | Large |
| 51-60% | 2d6 | Large |
| 61-70% | 1d8+1d6 | Huge |
| 71-80% | 2d8 | Huge |
| 81-90% | 1d10+1d8 | Enormous |
| 91-100% | 2d10 | Enormous |

Damage may be spread across multiple random Hit Locations; each additional location reduces damage by one row on the table. The number of potential locations is fixed at design time.

**Sample Trap -- Pitfall:**

- Purpose: Ensnaring or Death
- Trigger: Walking on the false surface covering the pit
- Difficulty: 60%
- Resistance: Evade to jump clear OR Hard Athletics to catch the edge
- Effect: 2d6 damage to a random Hit Location (armor does not protect); secondary effects at GM discretion.

**Sample Trap -- Poison Needle Trap:**

- Purpose: Ensnaring or Death
- Trigger: Attempting to pick the lock of the trapped object
- Difficulty: 60%
- Resistance: Endurance
- Effect: Poison needle springs into the picker's hand; GM selects poison type and effects.
