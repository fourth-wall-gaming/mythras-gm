**Awarding Experience Rolls:** GM discretion -- typically at the end of a scenario, story arc, or every 2-3 sessions. Guideline: **1-3 Experience Rolls per session** since last award.

**Increasing an Existing Skill** (costs 1 Experience Roll):

1. Roll 1d100 and add the character's INT.
2. If result >= current skill value: skill increases by **1d4+1%**.
3. If result < current skill value: skill increases by **1%**.
4. If the skill was Fumbled since the last award: free **+1%** (applied before the improvement roll). Multiple fumbles of the same skill do not stack.
5. A skill may only benefit from one Experience Roll per session.

**Increasing Passions:** Same procedure as increasing a skill. A Passion may also be reduced by an Experience Roll if supported by play (GM approval).

**Learning a New Professional Skill** (costs 3 Experience Rolls + in-game costs):

- Requires a source of knowledge (tutor, scroll, guild, etc.).
- Character must spend one full month of study and practice to reach Base Level.

**Learning New Class Abilities:**

- Cost = number of Experience Rolls equal to the character's current Rank.
- Many abilities are locked until a specific Rank is attained.
- No source of knowledge required (learned through repeated practice).

**Note:** Luck Points cannot be spent on Experience or Training rolls.
