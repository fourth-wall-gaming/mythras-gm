Build a character by working through nine steps:

1. **Concept:** Decide roughly what kind of character you want to play (cunning warrior, wily smuggler, studious academic). It need not be elaborate -- it guides later choices such as Culture and Career.

2. **Characteristics:** Determine the seven Characteristics -- STR, CON, SIZ, DEX, INT, POW, CHA -- by Dice Roll, Dice Roll Assign, Dice Roll High, or Points Build (pool of 80). Humans roll 3d6 for STR, CON, DEX, POW, CHA; 2d6+6 for SIZ and INT.

3. **Attributes:** Derive Action Points (all start with 2), Damage Modifier (STR+SIZ), Hit Points, Healing Rate, Initiative, Luck Points, Magic Points, and Movement Rate from the Characteristics.

4. **Skills:** Calculate Standard skill base values from Characteristic pairs (e.g. Athletics STR+DEX, Endurance CON x2). Customs and Native Tongue gain a static +40%.

5. **Culture:** Choose the society the character was raised in. The available cultures and their associated Standard/Professional skills come from the campaign's lore entries. Distribute 100 Culture skill points among the listed Standard skills and up to three chosen Professional skills (+1% per point).

6. **Career:** Choose the character's starting occupation. Available careers and their skills come from the campaign's lore entries. Distribute 100 Career skill points among listed Standard skills and up to three chosen Professional skills (+1% per point).

7. **Bonus Skill Points:** Assign a final pool of 150 points to any Standard skill or to Professional skills gained from Culture or Career. The GM may cap how many points go to any one skill.

8. **Passions:** Define the character's driving emotional ties and beliefs at their starting values.

9. **Equipment:** Gear the character with clothing appropriate to profession and setting, some pocket money, a personal heirloom or trinket, any trade tools and transportable equipment for the profession, a personal weapon for their Combat Style, and other setting-appropriate items subject to GM approval.
