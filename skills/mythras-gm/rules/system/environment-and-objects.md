### Fires

The Fire Intensity table gives five heat-damage intensities. Damage applies per Round: Intensity 1-2 sources hit a single Hit Location (the one touching the source); 3-4 hit the nearest 1d4+1 locations (radiant heat); 5 affects all locations simultaneously.

##### Fire Intensity Table

| Intensity | Examples | Time to Ignite | Damage |
| :-: | :-- | :-: | :-: |
| 1 | Candle | 1d4 | 1d2 |
| 2 | Torch | 1d3 | 1d4 |
| 3 | Campfire | 1d2 | 1d6 |
| 4 | Room-filling conflagration | 1d2 | 2d6 |
| 5 | Volcanic lava | Instant | 3d6 |

Fire-based attacks ignite flammable materials with a chance of damage rolled x5% (x1% for electrical). Once ignited, materials burn for 1d3-1 damage per Round until physically extinguished -- applied directly to Hit Points, ignoring Armor Points (natural armor still protects), and to flesh beneath. Drop prone and make a successful Athletics roll (1 Action Point, once per Turn) to smother flames; assistance or a location that self-extinguished makes the roll one grade easier.

### Game Time

- **Combat Rounds:** five seconds of real time; for short, frenetic, blow-by-blow activity.
- **Skill Time:** a few minutes to a few hours; deliberate effort with a definite result (picking a lock, scouting patrols).
- **Narrative Time:** time taken narrating or discussing; resembles real time during roleplay, compressed greatly during travel or downtime.

### Inanimate Objects

All objects have Armor Points and Hit Points; AP reduces damage before HP. At zero HP the object is useless. Breakage requires a Brawn, Unarmed, or Combat Style roll. Using a weapon against an object with AP equal to or greater than the weapon's damages both -- the weapon often breaks first (an axe vs an iron door). The GM decides which tools can affect which objects.
