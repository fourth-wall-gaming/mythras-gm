Diseases and poisons are handled identically for game purposes. Each manifests a number of traits:

- **Application:** how it enters the victim -- Ingestion (eat/drink), Inhaled (breathing/snorting), Contact (absorbed through skin), or Injected (piercing).
- **Potency:** virulence, set against a resisting skill in an Opposed Roll. Win and you shrug it off; lose and you suffer ALL of its Conditions at the appropriate times.
- **Resistance:** the resisting skill, usually Endurance or Willpower. Roll at exposure or defer to the Onset Time to conceal infection.
- **Onset Time:** delay before effect -- seconds, minutes, hours, or longer. Multiple effects may have different onset times.
- **Duration:** how long Conditions last.
- **Conditions:** one or more effects (below) suffered if resistance fails.
- **Antidote/Cure:** if treatable; success prevents further conditions but may leave enduring effects already inflicted.

##### Conditions Table

| Condition | Effects |
| :-- | :-- |
| Agony | Skill rolls using the affected area must also be <= Willpower or the attempt fails (victim moans/screams in pain). |
| Asphyxiation | Victim collapses incapacitated, unable to breathe (use Asphyxiation rules). May be asthmatic or fatal; the latter can be staved off by winning an Opposed First Aid vs Potency. |
| Bleeding | Internal or surface hemorrhaging causing Fatigue and recovery times per Fatigue / Blood Loss. |
| Contagious | Victim can transfer the toxin by touch. |
| Death | Victim collapses incapacitated and dies after CON rounds. |
| Fever | Body temperature fluctuates; all skills suffer a Hard difficulty grade. |
| Mania | Victim is driven to a compulsion (avoiding water, paranoia, self-mutilation, etc.) as noted. |
| Nausea | Cannot eat; roll Endurance on each stressful physical action or vomit for 1d3 rounds (cannot act). Long durations may starve. |
| Paralysis | Victim cannot move; affected location (or whole body) cannot be used for the duration. |
