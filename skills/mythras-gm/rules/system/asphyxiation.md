Characters can hold their breath for a number of seconds equal to their Endurance skill. The character must be prepared (filling the lungs with as much air as possible); if not, the period is halved if they were in a passive situation, or reduced to one-fifth if engaged in strenuous activity.

Once the held breath is over, the character must make an Endurance roll each Round:

- Critical Success: no further deterioration occurs.
- Success: the character accrues an extra level of Fatigue.
- Failure: the character sustains 1d2 extra levels of Fatigue that Round.
- Fumble: the character sustains 1d3 extra levels of Fatigue that Round.

Without aid, death from Asphyxiation is usually swift. If the Asphyxiation ends before the character dies, they recover Fatigue levels lost to suffocation relatively quickly, regaining one level per minute.

Depending on the method of Asphyxiation, the Games Master may prevent full recovery, imposing an enduring level of Fatigue to represent damage to the lungs caused by smoke or water inhalation.
