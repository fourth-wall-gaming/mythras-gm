Firearms add lethality that changes the combat paradigm. Bullets and laser beams travel faster than human reflexes, so unless a target dives clear using Evade, any hit immediately exposes the victim to one or more Special Effects. This also breaks the Action Point economy: if you cannot spend points to reactively defend, most characters spend all their actions firing, gambling on the enemy's incompetence. Game Masters and Players may need to rethink how they approach gun combat.

Key principles:
- Gun combat is dangerous.
- People prioritize not being shot over shooting others.
- The most common protection is to take cover.
- Suppressing fire is the best way to prevent foes from shooting back.
- Firearms are surprisingly inaccurate unless time is taken to brace and/or aim.
- When you are shot, bad stuff happens.

Defending against gunfire becomes a matter of body armor, using cover, and preventing the opponent from firing back. Most gun battles turn very tactical: suppressing fire, flanking movement, and flushing foes from deep cover. Characters may pass entire combat rounds pinned down and unable to respond until the opposition stops firing -- which some players find tedious.

An entire gunfight may produce no injuries, ending only when one side runs out of ammunition or flees. Few people want to risk being riddled by a weapon on full automatic. Throughout the combat rules, certain action types, Special Effects, and situations apply to firearms only, and are marked accordingly.
