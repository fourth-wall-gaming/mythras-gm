**Automatic Fire.** Self-chambering weapons offer three firing modes:
- *Semi-Automatic:* default rate; one shot per trigger pull, loads next round.
- *Burst:* a short number of rounds (usually 3-5) per pull. Imposes a Hard penalty to the attack roll. On success, roll a die to see how many rounds hit (a 3-round burst uses 1d3); roll separate location and damage for each hit.
- *Full-Automatic:* shooter declares rounds fired (up to cyclic rate), evenly distributed across all targets in the arc; spare rounds are lost traversing aim. A separate attack roll is made per target at Formidable difficulty; those hit suffer a random number of rounds as per Burst.

Only the first round to strike in a burst or full-auto spray can benefit from Special Effects (e.g. a critical permits Maximize Damage on the first shot only). Recoil penalties can be mitigated by Steady Weapon to brace.

**Dual Firearms.** Firing two weapons simultaneously: a pistol in each hand is Hard; a carbine or SMG in each hand is Formidable.

**Firing into a Crowd.** Firing at a target at a crowd's edge is Hard; firing through it is Formidable. Aiming may mitigate. If the shooter fails but would have succeeded without this penalty, an adjacent victim is struck instead (who may Evade or Parry). Multiple targets in line: determine the victim randomly. Special Effects apply only to the original target.

**Fumbling.** On a fumbled attack, the intended target selects a fumble-specific Special Effect that the shooter suffers (see Weapon Malfunction).

**Parrying with Shields.** You cannot deliberately parry a supersonic projectile or beam, but shields act as portable cover -- use Passive Blocking. For modern/sci-fi firearms, a GM may grant the shield Armor Points by material (e.g. wood 4 AP, Kevlar tactical shield 8 AP, force field 15 AP).

**Telescopic Sights.** Improve accuracy at range when the user takes time to Aim, easing skill rolls by one or more Grades depending on sophistication. No sight can produce an Automatic success; best attainable is Very Easy.
