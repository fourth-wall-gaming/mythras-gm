A Combat Style is a 'package' of multiple weapons related by their nature, learned together so the necessity of learning each weapon separately is avoided. The character learns to use each weapon both singly and in combination, interchanging them as needed, and to cope when placed at a severe disadvantage (weapon breakage, becoming disarmed).

A Hyperborean barbarian might know spear, hand axe, javelin, and shield; a Sumerian warrior shortsword, mace, shield, and bow. How many weapons fit in a style is decided by the Games Master and setting.

Characters using a weapon not covered by their style suffer a skill penalty of one or more difficulty grades.

Because each style is taught by a culture or career, it is optimised for the environment or tactics that group uses (fighting from the saddle, in close formation, etc.). A style may therefore be assigned one or more Traits granting an advantage under certain circumstances. Traits apply only to that style's weapons and situations; countless traits exist, many setting-specific.

##### Combat Style Traits Table

| Trait | Description |
| :-- | :-- |
| Beast-back Lancer | A mounted charge with this style does not incur the one-step difficulty penalty to hit. |
| Blind Fighting | Ignore penalties from poor lighting or temporary blinding. |
| Daredevil | Use Evade to dodge blows in hand-to-hand combat without ending up prone. |
| Defensive Minded | Increase your weapon's Size when parrying by one step, if no offensive action is taken that round. |
| Formation Fighting | An unflanked group of 3+ may draw into close formation, disadvantaging looser foes and reducing each engaging foe's Action Points by one. |
| Knockout Blow | When attacking with surprise, treat any Stun Location as lasting minutes instead of turns. |
| Mounted Combat | Ignore the skill cap placed on combat rolls by the Ride skill. |
| Ranged Marksman | With a ranged weapon, shift a random Hit Location roll to an adjoining location. |
| Skirmishing | Launch ranged attacks while walking or running. |
| Throw Weapons | Any melee weapon in the style can be thrown at no skill penalty, but damage is halved when thrown. |
| Unarmed Prowess | Treat Unarmed blocks/parries as 'Medium' sized to better defend against armed opponents. |
