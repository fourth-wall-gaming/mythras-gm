**Engagement:** A character is Engaged if within melee weapon range of an opponent -- only one of them need be able to strike the other. Once Engaged, a character cannot freely depart (charging past is an exception); breaking contact requires a deliberate act such as the Outmaneuver Action or the Withdraw Special Effect.

**Cover:** An attack landing on an obscured Hit Location is blocked to the extent of the cover's protection. Cover can be negated with Choose Location (aiming at visible parts) or by striking through penetrable cover. If cover is total but the attacker believes it will penetrate, they may strike blindly: the roll is one grade harder and Choose Location may not be taken.

**Evading:** Throwing oneself clear of an overwhelming attack -- an Opposed Roll of the defender's Evade vs the attacker's pertinent roll. Attacker wins: damage as normal. Defender wins: damage avoided. The winner with one or more Levels of Success may pick Special Effects. Whatever the result, the evader is left prone (unless they win the Arise effect).

**Knockback:** An attack imparting more damage than the recipient's SIZ (before parry/armor reduction) knocks them back. The recipient must pass an Easy Acrobatics or Standard Athletics roll to avoid falling prone, and is thrust one metre per five points (or fraction) of damage over their SIZ. The Brace Action can reduce or negate knockback.

**Leaping Attacks:** Resolved as an Opposed Roll of the leaper's Athletics vs the defender's Brawn or Evade (quadruped targets one grade harder). Leaper wins: defender is knocked prone with the attacker astride them; with Levels of Success, pick Special Effects. No damage from the leap itself; a prone victim cannot rise until the attacker leaves or they win an Arise effect. Works only against targets up to twice the attacker's SIZ. Brace makes the target harder to knock down.

**Outmaneuvering:** A character facing multiple foes uses movement to limit how many can attack, requiring room (not pinned). Resolve a group Opposed Roll of Evade skills; everyone spends an Action Point. Foes who fail to beat the maneuvering character cannot attack him that Round. If he beats all foes, he may safely engage a single foe for the Round or Withdraw entirely.

**Passive Blocking:** Holding a weapon (or shield) to cover a chosen location at the cost of being unable to actively Parry with it. Any attack hitting the covered locations automatically gets the weapon/shield's damage reduction. Choose Location cannot bypass the block. Crouching behind a shield doubles the locations covered.

**Surprise:** An unexpected attack on unaware opponents. The target suffers -10 Initiative; until their Initiative arrives they are flat-footed and cannot defend; the first successful attack gains a bonus Special Effect; for the rest of the Round they may not take offensive Actions.

**Sweep Attacks:** At the GM's decision, weapons or creatures of unusual size strike a clumped group with a single attack roll applied to all targets in the path. Each defender resolves the effects separately; Special Effects imposed on the attacker occur concurrently.
