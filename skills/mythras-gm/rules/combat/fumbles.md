**Parrying a Missed Attack:** If the attacker misses his initial attack roll, the defender may still spend an Action Point to Parry. Though it may seem pointless to parry an attack that would miss anyway, a skilled defender can use this to gain one or more Special Effects -- potentially weakening or incapacitating a foe -- while preserving their next turn for another Action.

**Unable or Unwilling to Parry:** If a defender cannot Parry (no Action Points remaining) or chooses not to (confident in weathering the blow), they are treated as having automatically rolled a Failure. This grants a successful attacker one or more Special Effects.

**Unsuccessful Rolls and Fumbles:** If both combatants Fail their rolls, or the defender declines to take advantage of a missed attack, the attack-Parry sequence ends and combat continues to the participant with the next highest Initiative.
